Popular Cities

Auckland

Auckland is New Zealand's largest, most cosmopolitan urban centre, beautifully framed by two sparkling harbours and a volcanic landscape of 53 dormant cones. Known as the "City of Sails," its unique geography blends high-end urban lifestyle with immediate access to natural marine playgrounds. Visitors can scale the iconic Sky Tower for unparalleled panoramic views, explore the chic eateries of the waterfront, or step back in time at the world-class collections inside the Auckland War Memorial Museum.

Wellington

Wellington, the nation's vibrant capital, is a compact coastal city celebrated as New Zealand's ultimate hub for arts, culture, and culinary excellence. Tucked tightly between a spectacular harbor and rolling green hills, it boasts a famously walkable downtown area bustling with independent fashion boutiques and experimental theatres. The city's crown jewel is Te Papa Tongarewa, the interactive national museum that showcases the country's rich Maori history, geological origins, and natural environment completely free of charge.

Christchurch

Christchurch is a beautifully transforming gateway city that masterfully combines traditional English heritage with bold, modern architectural design. Celebrated internationally as the "Garden City," its urban landscape is defined by expansive green spaces, including the expansive Hagley Park and the tranquil Christchurch Botanic Gardens. A signature experience here includes a classic punt ride down the serene Avon River, where a chauffeur gently guides you past weeping willows and elegant historic riverbanks.

Auckland

Just a short ferry ride across the Hauraki Gulf lies Waiheke Island, a world-famous haven of boutique vineyards, olive groves, and golden sand beaches. Auckland's West Coast offers a stark, wild contrast, where the dramatic black-sand beaches of Piha and Karekare attract surfers and hikers alike. It is the perfect starting point for any New Zealand itinerary, balancing city sophistication with raw coastal escape.


Auckland Zoo

Wildlife Adventure

$29 NZD / per person

Visiting Auckland Zoo involves exploring a 17-hectare parkland home to over 2,800 animals from 130 species. You can navigate themed habitats, including the award-winning South East Asia Jungle Track and Te Wao Nui, attend daily keeper talks, and learn about wildlife conservation while exploring habitats that showcase both native New Zealand species and animals from around the world.

Piha Beach and Rainforest Wilderness Experience

West Coast Adventure

$200 NZD / per person

Escape the bustling city into the lush native rain forest of the Waitakere Ranges. You'll stop at the Arataki Visitor Centre for panoramic views before heading to the rugged, black-sand surf beach of Piha. Enjoy a guided walk through the rain forest and view spectacular native flora, including towering Kauri trees.

Tikapa Moana Whales, Dolphins and Islands Cruise

Marine Wildlife Encounter

$170 NZD / per person

Set sail from downtown Viaduct Harbour into the Hauraki Gulf Marine Park. This interactive, marine-research-backed cruise allows you to spot wild whales, dolphins and rare seabirds against the backdrop of stunning island views. The on-board experts provide engaging commentary throughout the journey.



Wellington

Food and beverage enthusiasts will thrive in Wellington, which proudly claims more cafes and craft beer bars per capita than New York City. Film buffs can head to the coastal suburb of Miramar to explore the filmmaking magic of the Weta Workshop and see where Hollywood's biggest blockbusters come to life. With its passionate, creative community and dramatic coastal scenery, Wellington delivers a heavy dose of distinct Kiwi character.

Middle-earth Experience

$113 NZD / per person

Ideal for Tolkien fans, this tour takes you behind the scenes of Peter Jackson's iconic trilogies. You'll hike the Hobbiton Woods on Mt. Victoria, visit original filming locations, and enjoy a guided hands-on experience at the world-famous Weta Workshop

Zealandia By Day - Small Group Eco Tour

Native Wildlife Sanctuary

$70 NZD / per person

Discover one of the world's most successful urban wildlife sanctuaries on a guided eco-tour through Zealandia. Explore native forest habitats while learning about New Zealand's unique wildlife conservation efforts. Visitors may encounter native birds such as tui, kaka, kereru and takahe in a predator-free environment.

Half-Day Seal Coast Safari

Unique Landscapes

$195 NZD / per person

Journey along Wellington's rugged southern coastline in a 4WD vehicle and discover dramatic coastal landscapes. Visit remote beaches, learn about the region's history, and observe a thriving colony of New Zealand fur seals in their natural habitat.



Christchurch

Beyond its charming heritage, Christchurch is a launchpad for incredible South Island day trips, situated directly between the Pacific Ocean and the Southern Alps. Visitors can hop on the historic inner-city tramways to explore avant-garde shopping precincts, or head just two hours north to Kaikoura for world-class whale watching. It stands proudly as a resilient, forward-thinking metropolis where nature and modern architecture live in perfect harmony.


TranzAlpine & Arthur's Pass National Park Day Trip

Dramatic Scenery

$224 NZD / per person

Board the world-famous TranzAlpine train to journey through the Canterbury Plains before climbing into the Southern Alps. From there, a guided tour takes you exploring the massive limestone rock formations at Castle Hill and the roaring waterfalls of Arthur's Pass National Park.

Christchurch Tram, Gondola & River Punt Combo

Historic Charm

$122 NZD / per person

The ultimate city package combining Christchurch's best classic experiences. You can hop on and off a beautifully restored vintage heritage tramcar. Float down the tranquil Avon River on a classic handcrafted punt, and ride the Gondola high above the city for panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean.

Akaroa Harbour Nature Cruise

Wildlife Wonders

$130 NZD / per person

Travel outside the city to the historic French settlement of Akaroa. Board a catamaran cruise into a volcanic harbour to spot rare Hector's dolphins, alongside fur seals, penguins and sea birds.
