Welcome To New Zealand

Welcome to Travel NZ, your ultimate gateway to New Zealand, A land of unparalleled natural beauty, vibrant indigenous culture, and legendary outdoor adventure. From the snow-capped alpine speaks of the South Island to the geothermal wonders and golden coastlines of the North, this breathtaking country offers an unforgettable journy for every type of traveller. Whether you are seeking a serene escape into ancient forests or a thrilling leap into adrenaline-pumping sports, New Zealand promises an immersive experience that will capture your heart and inspire your soul.

Top 5 Things To Do in New Zealand

1. Milford Sound

Known as the eighth wonder of the world, this breathtaking fjord features towering waterfalls, sheer rock faces, and resident fur seals and dolphins. Carved by ancient glaciers within New Zealand's Fiordland National Park, the dramatic landscapes of Milford Sound / Piopiotahi come alive during heavy rain, when hundreds of temporary cascades multiply down the vertical cliffs.

2. Rotorua's Geothermal Wonders

Experience bubbling mud pools, natural hot springs, erupting geysers, and traditional Maori Cultural performances. Nestled directly on the Pacific Rim of Fire, the volcanic wonderland of Rotorua stands as the beating cultural heart of New Zealand's North Island. Visitors to Te Puia can stand in awe before the mighty Pohutu Geyser, the Southern Hemisphere's largest active geyser, which shoots plumes of scalding water up to 30 metres into the sky.

3. Hobbiton

Tour the pristine, real-life movie set from The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit trilogies nestled in rural Matamata. Set against the backdrop of a working Alexander family sheep farm, this iconic 12-acre location transports fans directly into the whimsical heart of The Shire. Visitors can stroll along the vibrant, flower-lined pathways past 44 intricately detailed Hobbit Holes, each adorned with tiny gates, firewood piles, and miniature garden tools that make the village feel fully inhabited.

4. Queenstown

Push your limits in the official adventure capital of the world, home to the historic Kawarau Bridge bungy jump. Ringed by the formidable, snow-capped peaks of the Remarkables mountain range and positioned on the edge of the crystal-clear Lake Wakatipu, Queenstown serves as a magnet for thrill-seekers from every corner of the globe. At the AJ Hackett Kawarau Bungy Centre, brave travellers can relive the dawn of extreme sports by plunging 43 metres toward the rushing turquoise waters below, even opting for a refreshing river touch.

5. Bay of Islands

Discover an aquatic playground of 144 subtropical islands, rich in early maritime history and home to playful bottlenose dolphins. Located in the winterless north of New Zealand, this stunning marine park serves as a haven for yachting, world-class big-game fishing, and secluded beach escapes. Sailing through the sheltered, turquoise waters often brings face-to-face encounters with diverse marine life, from whales and fur seals to the region's famous coastal birds.
